LANTERN

(Unreal Engine 4) Summer 2017 - Now: lead engineer
YouTube (VERY OLD PROTOTYPE)

  • Created the original prototype version of Lantern as a solo engineer as a proof of concept to pass the competitive USC AGP judging process
  • Managed a 6-person engineering team in a 25-person team to create a virtual reality puzzle game for the Oculus Rift, under the USC AGP program.
  • Planned and assigned weekly tasks to all engineers (including myself) in a way that optimized efficiency by analyzing the strengths/weaknesses of engineers and prevented burnout by alternating between features/polish.
  • Planned and created a virtual reality toolkit from the ground up, that handled everything from basic interactions to hand gestures/animations, to meet the specific needs of a haptic VR puzzle game.
  • Naturally a very 3D math heavy game, since virtual reality gives us raw 3D world locations/rotations as opposed to traditional boolean or 1D inputs.

    • COLLECTATHON

    (Unreal Engine 4) - programmer, gameplay designer, animator, UI designer, sound engineer
    YouTube

  • Collaborated on a 3-person team to design core gameplay for a 3D third-person action platformer. Created and programmed all animations as well as various weapons and systems in the game.

    • KITCHEN STRIKE

    (Unreal Engine 4) Fall 2016: solo project
    (Backed up) Website

  • Worked on an online multiplayer shooter game in which each team owns its own restaurant. Each team attempts to pay rent by the end of the day via operating their restaurant, but they also seek to bankrupt the other team's restaurant through sabotage or combat.
  • It's supposed to be a realistic restaurant simulator where the player has to either manually perform all the duties of a restaurant (cooking, serving, cleaning) or at least hire an NPC to do it for them. At the same time you can mess with the opposing restaurants through knocking down their trash cans, breaking their equipment, or by simply attacking their players/employees. I never got to finish this project, but I released a playable version by the end of the summer.
